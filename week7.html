Week 7

This was the first week at the Telstra Creator Space. I've always ever only walked by this space before, wondering what it could all be. Finally through this subject I get to step foot inside! I've been looking forward to entering this space, unsure of what is waiting ahead of me, but boy oh boy was I in for an adventure.

Introduction to physical computing

The lecture introduced us to physical technology that serve not only as functional devices, but also as artistic pieces ( I now realise how much I enjoy watching them) This reminds me of my own interaction with physical technology.

Back when I was on a cruise, there was a robotic bartender where we could almost customize our drink order and watch the robot make it through its automated process. The drink? Terrible. The entertainment value? 10/10. As an ex bartender, that cocktail broke my heart. But would I still pay for that $200 experience? YES.

What I liked the most about this experience was feeling like I collaborated with this machine to get my drink. Also watching it mimic the full human range of motion through its bionic arms really puts into perspective how much movement and coordination we go through daily without thinking much of.

Getting started with Arduino

We were split into pairs and given a Arduino kit to disect.

This is my first time seeing and let alone holding a physical computing kit. I was completely lost and unsure of what I was even looking at. Thankfully the first task, to guess what each part was, was done as pair work... it brought comfort knowing my partner was equally as confused. But nevertheless we tried our best trying to guess what each piece was based on its look and size. Needless to say, we got some horribly wrong, but I'm proud to announce we got the battery right... at the very least.

Building a push button LED setup

The first set up was to set up a button that activated the LED when pressed. Although now looking back, the idea and execution was simple, but in that moment I had no idea what I was looking at and how to follow the Tinkercad example.

Eventually after lots of tinkering and getting help from Matilda and Karen, we eventually managed to get it working.

Getting Arduino to beep

After getting a hang of the first set up, this second task did not feel as hard. I liked that we got to try out another tool -which looks like speakers too! How cute

Overall thoughts

This was a great first introduction to physical computing. The lecture session showed us the great capabilities of physical tech and got me excited thinking about how I can also start thinking about artistic technology within my own practice.

The hands on session was trickly, challenging but rewarding. Nothing beats seeing our LED light up, or sound coming out. However the one thing I did find especially challenging was how small the whole set up was and how soft it all felt. I had trouble getting the wires in the exact hole sometimes and getting that wrong caused my device to not work.
